What is Fashion Product Lifecycle Management (PLM)?

12 min read 8th Oct, 2023

Introduction

The idea that a product has life “stages” is at least as old as the 1930s. In fact, an ad agency employee in 1957 is believed to be the first to theorize the five-stage product life cycle, which included introduction, growth, maturity, saturation and finally, decline.

Today, Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) is well known as the comprehensive process that covers every stage of a product’s journey—from the initial spark of an idea, through design and production, to its final phase of retirement or reuse. The exact stages may differ from industry to industry, but PLM, broadly, is a process that harmonizes all aspects of product management, including manufacturing and marketing, that help ensure consistency and efficiency across the board.

In the fashion industry, where trends shift in the blink of an eye, managing the lifecycle of a product effectively can be a mammoth task. PLM systems are crucial for helping brands scale and grow. This blog explores the stages of fashion product lifecycles, how they can be managed effectively, and the digital solutions, such as PIM, PDM, ERP, and PLM, that help fashion brands manage their development processes.

What is PLM in Fashion Industry?

Fashion businesses operate within a highly fragmented and complex supply chain, characterized by rapid design cycles, tight deadlines, and changing consumer tastes. The pressure to bring new collections to market quickly, while maintaining quality and staying ahead of competitors, makes PLM solutions indispensable.

A PLM System for fashion integrates the work of various teams, including design, development, sourcing, production, and marketing—so that everyone is on the same page, reducing errors, accelerating time-to-market, and ultimately driving profitability.

This alignment is particularly crucial in fashion, where even the slightest delay or miscommunication can lead to significant setbacks, from missed trends to unsold inventory. A good PLM centralizes and streamlines workflows, enabling brands to quickly respond to market demands and consumer preferences, ensuring that the right product reaches the right customer at the right time and at the right price.
